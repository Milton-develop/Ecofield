How to use EcoField Logger effectively during fieldwork

Logging Observations

  • Go to the Log page.
  • Fill in all required fields accurately.
  • Use the correct Group ID assigned to your group.
  • Click Submit Observation to save your entry.

Viewing Group Data (Data Page)

  • Click Data in the navbar.
  • Enter your Group ID and Password to log in.
  • Only your group’s observations will be visible.
  • You can Logout to end your session; this resets the page and requires password again.
  • After fieldwork, data can be archived by the admin for reuse of group IDs next year.

Downloading Group Data

  • After logging in, click Download Filtered CSV.
  • Only your group’s data will be downloaded.
  • The file is saved in CSV format for easy analysis.

Using the Eco-Stats Dashboard

  • Access: Click the Eco-Stats Dashboard link above the form to open the visualization tool.
  • Live Analysis: The dashboard automatically reads your submitted logs and updates charts in real-time.
  • Key Metrics:
    • Simpson's Index: Automatically calculates biodiversity (0 to 1 scale) to determine ecosystem health.
    • Habitat Comparison: View side-by-side graphs comparing species abundance in Forests, Grasslands, etc.
  • Troubleshooting: If the dashboard is empty, ensure you have submitted at least one observation in the Logger first.

Managing Groups (Admin Only)

  • The Admin Dashboard allows adding new group IDs and passwords.
  • Admins can archive data at the end of fieldwork to allow reuse of group numbers next year.
  • All credentials are stored securely in groups.csv.

Best Practices

  • Double-check species names for accuracy.
  • Use the notes field for behavior or unusual sightings.
  • Submit observations promptly after fieldwork.
  • Do not share your group password with other groups.
  • Admins should archive data only after the fieldwork period is complete.
